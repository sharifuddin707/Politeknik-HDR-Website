What is our Role in Politeknik Brunei?

Human Resource Department covers the two areas: Administration duties & Human Resource Management.

Administration duties

The human resource specialist generally serves as the liaison between teachers, staff and school administrators. Including:

Budget:
  1. Updating the occupancy of any available post for recruiting employee on a monthly basis .
Correspondence:
  1. Internally:
    • Managing the curriculum vitae and related documents of a new employee and assign them in appropriate departments according to their expertise.
    • Managing appointment and interviews in Politeknik Brunei.
  2. Externally:
    • Update change of post designation to confer new position for Politeknik Brunei staff to Public Service Commission.
Recruitment:
    • Communicate with related agencies find valuable employees as assets that will be beneficial to Politeknik Brunei.
Rank promotion:
  1. Determine if the employee deserve a promotion to a higher rank and salary depending on their work performance and behaviour.
  2. Nominate The Head of School and Head of Department on a yearly basis.
  3. Keep in contact with the Ministry of Education regarding the elected Head of School and Head of Department.
Appraisal:
  1. Determine if the employees annual work performance meet up the expectation of Politeknik Brunei.

Human Resource Management:

Identify training providers within and outside the country.
Communicate with training providers from within and outside the country.
Disseminate training information to relevant departments.
Identify and nominate the officers and staff for training.
Submit training application for approval from Permanent Secretary of Higher Education.
Liaise with representatives of Brunei Darussalam Foreign Missions for:
  1. Accommodation
  2. Transportation
  3. Payment of programme
Issue minute for the officers and staff who will attend training to submit reports and to conduct sharing sessions on their completed training.
Manage long-term training/meeting/seminar/conference locally and abroad (In-Service Training):
  1. Circulate information about the courses offered and their datelines.
  2. Review application forms.
  3. Establish an Ad Hoc committee to shortlist the candidates.
  4. Extend application to the Ministry of Education.
  5. Provide feedback on the outcome of the application.
